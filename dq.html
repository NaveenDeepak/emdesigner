Convention, equations for PMSM

magnet flux-linkage

\(\lambda_{am} = -\lambda_m \cos(\theta)\)
\(\lambda_{bm} = -\lambda_m \cos(\theta - 2 \pi/3)\)
\(\lambda_{cm} = -\lambda_m \cos(\theta + 2 \pi/3)\)

emf

\(e_a = E \sin(\theta)\)
\(e_b = E \sin(\theta - 2 \pi/3)\)
\(e_c = E \sin(\theta + 2 \pi/3)\)

self-inductance

\(Laa = L_0 + L_2 \cos(2 \theta)\)
\(Lbb = L_0 + L_2 \cos(2 \theta + 2 \pi/3)\)
\(Laa = L_0 + L_2 \cos(2 \theta - 2 \pi/3)\)

mutual-inductance

\(Lab = M_0 + M_2 \cos(2\theta - 2\pi/3)\)
\(Lbc = M_0 + M_2 \cos(2\theta)\)
\(Lca = M_0 + M_2 \cos(2\theta + 2\pi/3)\)

\(L_{abc} = \begin{bmatrix} Laa & Lab & Lca \\ Lab & Lbb & Lbc \\ Lca & Lbc & Lca \end{bmatrix}\)

transformation matrix

\(K = \dfrac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix} \cos(\theta) & \cos(\theta-2\pi/3) & \cos(\theta+2\pi/3)\\ \sin(\theta) & \sin(\theta-2\pi/3) & \sin(\theta+2\pi/3)\\ 0.5 & 0.5 & 0.5 \end{bmatrix}\)

inverse transformation matrix

\(K^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(\theta) & \sin(\theta) & 1 \\ \cos(\theta-2\pi/3) & \sin(\theta-2\pi/3) & 1 \\ \cos(\theta+2\pi/3) & \sin(\theta+2\pi/3) & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)

To obtain dq0 parameters, use the following transformations

\(I_{dq0} = K I_{abc}\)

\(V_{dq0} = K V_{abc}\)

\(L_{dq0} = K L_{abc} K^{-1}\)

Ldq0 axis inductance

\(L_d = (L_0 - M_0) - (\dfrac{L_2}{2} + M_2)\)

\(L_q = (L_0 - M_0) + (\dfrac{L_2}{2} + M_2)\)

\(L_{dq0}\) measurements

\(L_d, L_q\) can be measured in practice by locking the direct axis of rotor to one of the phases. Example:

L0, L2, M0, M2, ta = sympy.symbols('L0, L2, M0, M2, ta')
laa = L0 + L2*sympy.cos(2*ta)
lbb = L0 + L2*sympy.cos(2*ta + 2*sympy.pi/3)
lcc = L0 + L2*sympy.cos(2*ta - 2*sympy.pi/3)
lab = M0 + M2*sympy.cos(2*ta -2*sympy.pi/3)
lbc = M0 + M2*sympy.cos(2*ta)
lca = M0 + M2*sympy.cos(2*ta +2*sympy.pi/3)

Procedure 1 for measuring \(L_d, L_q\)

  1. short the terminals B-C
  2. pass dc current in through phase-A and out of shorted terminals of phase-B/C
  3. pass sufficient current to ensure that rotor moves and locks to phase A
  4. After the rotor locks itself to phase-A. Stop applying DC current and remove the connections to current source. With phase B and C still connected, measure inductance \(L_m\) across phase-A and shorted phase-B/C terminal

The measured inductance is given as

Lm = laa + lbb/2 -lab*3/2 - lca/2 + lbc/2
Lm

\(\displaystyle \frac{3 L_{0}}{2} - \frac{L_{2} \sin{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{6} \right)}}{2} + L_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta \right)} - \frac{3 M_{0}}{2} + \frac{M_{2} \sin{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{6} \right)}}{2} + \frac{M_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta \right)}}{2} + \frac{3 M_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{3} \right)}}{2}\)

According to the expressions for inductance. rotor position \(ta\) is 90 degrees when the direct axis is aligned to Phase A. Substituting the same, expression of measured inductance is calculated as 3/2 times the Ld value.

Lm.evalf(subs={ta: np.pi/2})

\(\displaystyle 1.5 L_{0} - 0.75 L_{2} - 1.5 M_{0} - 1.5 M_{2}\)

Above expression is \(\dfrac{3}{2}L_d\), providing experimental values for \(L_d\).
In this rotor position, inductance (\(L_m\)) measured between phase B and phase C is

lm = lbb + lcc - 2*lbc
lm

\(\displaystyle 2 L_{0} - L_{2} \sin{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{6} \right)} - L_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{3} \right)} - 2 M_{0} - 2 M_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta \right)}\)

Substituting the same rotor position angle for \(ta\) shows that the measured values is twice the Lq inductance

lm.evalf(subs={ta: np.pi/2})

\(\displaystyle 2.0 L_{0} + 1.0 L_{2} - 2.0 M_{0} + 2.0 M_{2}\)

Above expression is \(2L_q\), providing experimental value for \(L_q\)

Procedure 2 for measuring \(L_d, L_q\)

  1. Pass dc current through any two terminals. Example: phase-B (+ve) and phase-C (-ve)
  2. Pass sufficient current to ensure that rotor moves and locks to a fixed position
  3. After the rotor locks itself. Remove DC current and measure inductance \(L_m\) across phase-B and phase-C

Inductance measurement between B and C

lm = lbb + lcc - 2*lbc
lm

\(\displaystyle 2 L_{0} - L_{2} \sin{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{6} \right)} - L_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{3} \right)} - 2 M_{0} - 2 M_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta \right)}\)

In the above example, the rotor q-axis aligns with phase-A axis. The rotor position \(ta\) is 0 degrees.
substituting the same for calculating \(L_m\)

lm.evalf(subs={ta: 0})

\(\displaystyle 2.0 L_{0} - L_{2} - 2.0 M_{0} - 2.0 M_{2}\)

above expression is \(2L_d\), providing an experimental value for \(L_d\)

Inductance measurement between A and B or A and C does not provide other useful information

# measurement between a and b
lm = laa + lbb - 2*lab
lm

\(\displaystyle 2 L_{0} - L_{2} \sin{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{6} \right)} + L_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta \right)} - 2 M_{0} + 2 M_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{3} \right)}\)

lm.evalf(subs={ta: 0})

\(\displaystyle 2.0 L_{0} + 0.5 L_{2} - 2.0 M_{0} + M_{2}\)

# measurement between a and c
lm = laa + lcc - 2*lca
lm

\(\displaystyle 2 L_{0} + L_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta \right)} - L_{2} \cos{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{3} \right)} - 2 M_{0} + 2 M_{2} \sin{\left(2 ta + \frac{\pi}{6} \right)}\)

lm.evalf(subs={ta: 0})

\(\displaystyle 2.0 L_{0} + 0.5 L_{2} - 2.0 M_{0} + M_{2}\)
